Cybersecurity Information

Learn the best cyber strategies to keep your digital information safe online.

Pre-Quiz Explanation

Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity ensures that our digital information and technical systems are protected, so we can use them safely. It also prohibits unpermitted access to private information.

Password Creation

How To Make A Strong Password: A strong password should be unique, long, a mix of characters, numbers and symbols, and hard to crack!

CyberCriminals

An individual that uses digital technology to commit illegal activities, no matter how small.

Cyber Security Warnings

They inform people on security threats.Cybersecurity Advisories & Guidance increases awareness on cyber attacks.

2FA

2FA stands for two-factor authentication. It provides alternatives to your secret password code for extra protection or backup recovery. Like fingerprinting, or an additional contact phone number

Quiz Child

Think Before You Share Online

Sharing personal information on social media, such as your home address, phone number, or financial details, can make you an easy target for identity theft, scams, and other security risks

Download Games Safely!

The safest way to download a game is from a trusted source, like an official app store (Google Play, Apple App Store) or the developer’s website

Don't Fall for Tricky Prize Messages

Messages claiming you've won a prize and asking you to click a link are often scams or phishing attempts

Be Careful with Strange Emails

Emails from unknown senders asking for personal information are often phishing attempts. Clicking on links or providing your details could compromise your account

Secure Browsing

A website starting with "https://" and showing a padlock icon indicates it uses encryption to protect your data. The "s" in "https" stands for "secure," meaning any information you enter, such as login details or payment information, is encrypted and less vulnerable

Quiz Adult

Recognize Phishing Attempts

It’s always best to ignore emails that seem suspicious, especially if they contain errors. Contacting your bank directly using verified contact information is the safest approach.

Assess Website Credibility

Researching the website before making a purchase is a good way to protect yourself from potential scams. A lack of reviews or unprofessional appearance can be a red flag.

Handle Ransomware Attacks Responsibly

Disconnecting your device from the internet helps prevent further damage. It’s best to seek professional help rather than paying the ransom, as there’s no guarantee the attacker will release your files.

Use Unique Passwords for Each Account

Using unique passwords for each account is essential for security. When one account is compromised, unique passwords protect your other accounts from being hacked.

Avoid Bluetooth Vulnerabilities

Leaving Bluetooth open can expose your device to unauthorized connections. Attackers nearby may exploit Bluetooth to gain access to your device.

Quiz Elderly

Back-up Recovery Safety

If you suspect your login information was compromised do not click unknown links, reset your password, sign out of all accounts, alert your emailing company, look for any suspicious activity and backup your information using 2 factor authentication.

Unknown Call Safety

If you want to figure out if you personally know the unknown caller the safest thing to do is ask who this is and the reason for the call. Avoid responding with the word yes because scammers can use this trick to record your voice and use it for voice recognition purposes. If you feel uncomfortable about the response, hang up and block the number.

Remote Control Scams

Remote control scams are people who trick callers into giving them a code to control their personal device by telling the user to download an app. Be cautious and double check the number you are calling with the Best Buy number from their official website contact us page, employees will not ask you to download an app to control your device.

Unknown Message Safety

If you receive a text from an unknown number asking you to click a link and send something this is a fraud scam. DO NOT click the link, Do NOT respond to any messages, Do delete and block the number.

Phishing Attempt Safety

Do not share your login credentials with anyone claiming to be a tech employee. They will never ask you to share this information via text or email! Contact your tech support team using their official website services, check if you can login and delete and/or block the number.
